Essential Travel Tips

Make your visit to India's monuments a safe, respectful, and memorable experience.

Before You Go

  • Check Timings: Many monuments have specific visiting hours and are closed one day a week (e.g., the Taj Mahal is closed on Fridays).
  • Book Online: Book your tickets in advance on the official ASI website to avoid long queues and get a small discount.
  • Research Rules: Some sites have strict rules about bags, cameras, food, and drones. Check beforehand to avoid hassles.

What to Pack

  • Comfortable Footwear: You'll be doing a lot of walking. Comfortable shoes are a must.
  • Sun Protection: Carry a hat, sunglasses, and sunscreen, as the Indian sun can be intense.
  • Water Bottle: Stay hydrated! Carry a reusable water bottle. Most major sites have refilling stations.

On-Site Etiquette

  • Dress Modestly: When visiting active places of worship (temples, mosques), cover your shoulders and knees. You may need to remove your shoes.
  • Respect the Site: Do not touch or lean on delicate carvings. Never write on walls. Help preserve our heritage.
  • Hire Official Guides: For the best experience, hire government-approved guides available at the ticket counter.

Health & Safety

  • Be Aware: Be mindful of your belongings in crowded places.
  • Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water, especially during hot weather, to avoid heatstroke.
  • Follow Pathways: Stick to designated paths and be careful on uneven surfaces or steep steps.
